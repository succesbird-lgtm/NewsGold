Gold Curiosities

Fascinating facts about gold that you probably didn't know

🌍

Total Gold Ever Mined

~212,582 tonnes

If all the gold ever mined were melted into a single cube, it would measure just 23 metres on each side — small enough to fit inside an Olympic swimming pool.

⚛️

Gold Comes From Space

Billions of years old

Gold was not created on Earth. It was formed in neutron star collisions (kilonovas) billions of years ago and arrived on Earth via asteroid impacts early in the planet's history.

🏦

US Gold Reserves

8,133 tonnes

The United States holds the world's largest official gold reserve, stored primarily at Fort Knox, Kentucky. At current prices, it is worth over $500 billion USD.

📱

Gold in Your Phone

~0.03 grams

Every smartphone contains a tiny amount of gold in its circuit board. Around 300 tonnes of gold are used in electronics manufacturing globally each year.

🦷

The Oldest Use

6,000+ years

Gold has been used by humans for at least 6,000 years. The earliest gold artefacts were found in the Varna Necropolis in Bulgaria, dating to around 4,500 BC.

🌊

Gold in the Ocean

~20 million tonnes

The world's oceans contain an estimated 20 million tonnes of dissolved gold. The challenge is that extracting it is currently far too expensive to be economically viable.

🏆

Olympic Gold Medals

~6 grams of gold

Despite being called "gold medals," Olympic gold medals are actually mostly silver. They contain just about 6 grams of gold plating over a silver core — a requirement since 1912.

🔬

Gold is Edible

E175

Pure gold is biologically inert and safe to eat. It is approved as a food additive (E175) in many countries and used to decorate luxury foods, desserts, and beverages.
