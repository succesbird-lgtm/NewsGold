Over 1 Million People in Line for Bitcoin Trading App

By Maria Silva (Grade 12, Student Author) • March 5, 2025 • 4 min read • Supervised by Prof. João Oliveira
📱

Bitcoin is a cryptocurrency and worldwide payment system. It is the first decentralised digital currency, as the system works without a central bank or single administrator. The network is peer-to-peer, and transactions take place between users directly, without an intermediary. These transactions are verified by network nodes and recorded in a public distributed ledger called a blockchain.

"Bitcoin represents a fundamental shift in how we think about money, value, and the role of centralised institutions in our financial lives." — Financial Technology Review

What is Bitcoin?

Bitcoin was invented by an unknown person or group of people under the name Satoshi Nakamoto and released as open-source software in 2009. Bitcoins are created as a reward for a process known as mining. They can be exchanged for other currencies, products, and services. As of February 2015, over 100,000 merchants and vendors accepted bitcoin as payment. Research produced by the University of Cambridge estimated that in 2017, there were 2.9 to 5.8 million unique users actively using a cryptocurrency wallet, most of them using bitcoin.

The Trading App Phenomenon

The latest bitcoin trading application has attracted over one million users to its waitlist within just 48 hours of its announcement. This unprecedented demand highlights the growing mainstream interest in cryptocurrency trading, particularly among younger generations who are more comfortable with digital financial instruments.

Why is This Important for Financial Education?

Understanding how digital currencies work is becoming an essential part of financial literacy. For students entering the workforce or higher education, familiarity with blockchain technology, decentralised finance, and digital assets provides a competitive advantage in an increasingly digital economy.

The rise of cryptocurrency also illustrates fundamental economic concepts such as supply and demand — Bitcoin has a fixed maximum supply of 21 million coins — scarcity, speculation, and the relationship between technology and financial markets.

Risks and Considerations

As with all investments, cryptocurrency comes with significant risks. Bitcoin's price is notoriously volatile, and investors have experienced dramatic gains and losses. Regulation around cryptocurrency varies by country and is constantly evolving. Students and investors should always conduct thorough research and consider seeking advice from qualified financial professionals before making any investment decisions.
